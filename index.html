Achrondroplasia

Today you will learn about a genetic disorder called Achrondoplasia. Scroll down to Learn More.

Achrondroplasia is a genetic disorder that is hereditary. But, it can also be obtained through genetic mutations. This disorder is a bone growth disorder that causes disproportionate dwardism. Dwarfism is the condition of short stature as an adult.

Symptoms of Achrondroplasia are Physical deformity, pain in the back, short arms and legs, enlarged head, an average sized trunk, short stature, bow legs, excessive inward curvature of the lower back, short fingers, englarged head, hunched back, middle ear infection, misaligned teeth, and snoring.

There are a few Treatment Options of Achrondoplasia some of these treatment options include, surgery and taking hormones to increase height slightly.

Achrondroplasia affects the body in many ways but there are some main body systems that are affects by this disorder are the skeletal and muscular systems.

Achrondroplasia is a very rare condition and around 20,00 people a year are diagnosed with Achrondroplasia.

The Phenotype and Genotype that makes up the disorder is the dominant allele that codes for a mutation in the growth factor gene 3 (FGFR3.)
